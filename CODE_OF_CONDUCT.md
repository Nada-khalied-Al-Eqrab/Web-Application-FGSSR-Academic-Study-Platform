# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct 📜

## 1. Our Pledge
We, as students, researchers, and developers of the **FGSSR Academic Study Platform**, pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free and inclusive experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community aligned with the academic integrity of **Cairo University**.

---

## 2. Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our academic project include:
* **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** towards other team members and reviewers.
* **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and architectural preferences.
* **Giving and gracefully receiving constructive feedback** regarding code implementation, UML diagrams, or database optimizations.
* **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience.
* **Focusing on what is best** for the academic institution (FGSSR) and the success of the graduation project.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* Public or private harassment of any form.
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, student ID numbers, or phone numbers, without explicit permission.
* Plagiarism, code theft, or academic dishonesty that violates Cairo University's internal regulations.

---

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

The project leads (the core development team under the supervision of **Prof. Dr. Shahira Azazy**) are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate, fair, and corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project leads have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code changes, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (both local development environments and online repositories), and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces or internal university academic presentations.

---

## 5. Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

The project team will maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of any incident.
